Expedition to Baruntse 7129 m





The Baruntse with Mera Peak


The Baruntse is located between the valleys of Makalu and Khumbu. The access trek starts from Lukla and goes up the Pangum valley in the direction of Mera Peak whose climb will acclimatize to the altitude.

The base camp is next to Lake Baruntse around 5400 m. The ascent passes by Col West then the ascent to the camp 2 towards 6500 m is done by wide slopes whose inclination is weak.

Camp 2 at the top is first wide slopes inclined about 35 ° then the slope is recovering to reach 45 °. Then a succession of small rather steep projections and a narrow ridge (large ledges) lead to the summit.

For better acclimatization to the altitude possibility not to leave Lukla but Phaplu.

Camp C1 at West Pass at 6100 m.


From the base camp on the lake at 5400 m the path to the foot of the corridor leading to the West Pass is very steep.



The departure of the corridor is done in the rocks right bank then it is a slope of snow between 35 ° and 40 ° on 250 mwhich leads to the camp 1 located just behind the pass with a splendid sight on the Makalu and the crossing of the 3 Passes between Khumbu and Makalu.




Camp 2 at 6500 m.

From camp 1 we avoid some big visible crevasses. The climb without being steep is supported until the plateau on which is installed C2.

The next climb to the summit which begins with a small slope at 30 ° and then a crossing we reach another slope of about 200 m of uneven to reach the rating 7000 m.

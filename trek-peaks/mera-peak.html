Trekking Mera Peak





Ascent of Mera Peak 6470 m


Climbing Mera Peak alone is not technically difficult. The Mera Peak trekking loop is a good start for a first summit of more than 6000 m.
We organize the circuits from Lukla by Puyang and Pangum to avoid the Zatrav pass The far too high from the 2nd day. This allows to better acclimatize to the altitude by a gradual climb in altitude.
The ascent of Mera Peak requires 1 high camp at High Camp at 5800 m.
TThe other 2 Mera treks by Amphulapsa and Mera and Island Peak are done with the return by the magnificent Amphulapcha pass at 5850 m which allows to reach the Khumbu valley with views of Nuptse, Ama Dablam, Makalu etc.
The crossing of the Amphulapsa Pass requires good skills in mountaineering.

At the top of the Mera Peak, a magnificent view of the Himalayan 8000m range from Kangchenjunga, Makalu, Everest, Lhotse to Cho Oyu.
This crossing is a beautiful trip in altitude that will take you through 3 different valleys. That of Tangnag Kharé then that of Baruntse Amphulapsa to end with that of Khumbu Namche.

Difficulty climbing Mera Peak alone


The ascent of Mera Peak is not technically difficult. However, she claims a good physical condition and a bivouac at 5700 m is necessary. The final slope is inclined at about 35 ° -40 ° and 50 m high and equipped with a fixed rope.

You must be able to cling to the ascent or descent in slopes of about 40 °, know how to use a descender, to walk roped, know how to use a jumar, know how to self-insure and ensure a second, etc.

You must be equipped with all your mountaineering equipment: metal cleats, ice ax, shoulder belt, 1 ice pin, 2 self closing snap hooks, jumar, cord, frontal, a survival blanket, etc.



Difficulty climbing Mera Peak with crossing Amphulapsa and Island Peak


Island Peak and the crossing of the Amphulaptsa Pass are glacier high mountain routes. Real mountaineering skills and a very good physical condition are essential. The equivalent is crossing the Trois Mont Blanc.

It is necessary to be able to cling to the ascent and descent in slopes of about 45 ° to about 200 m in height, to know how to use a descender, to walk roped, to know how to use a jumar, to know how to ensure and provide a second, etc.

You must be equipped with all its mountaineering equipment: metal cleats, ice ax, shoulder belt, 2 ice pins, 2 self closing snap hooks, jumper, cord, mountain helmet for the Amphulapsa Pass and the Island Peak, frontal, survival blanket, etc.

The crossing of the Col de l'Amphulapsa is the most technical and delicate part.

Trekking Peak Island Peak



Ascension Island Peak 6180 m

Island Peak is located at the foot of the southern face of Lhoste in the Sherpa Khumbu country.
For the Peak Peak trekking we will leave Lukla at 2800 m, which we will reach by plane. We will acclimatize to the altitude by going up to Renjo Pass then to Gokyo Peak at 5360 m and finally to Kala Patthar at 5550 m.
As soon as we are acclimated to the altitude, it will be the ascent of the Island Peak at 6189m. Views of the most beautiful mountains in the world, Ama Dablam, Everest, Pumori, Lhotse etc.
For this climb we install a base camp at 5100 m. The next day from very early is the day of the summit.
The sequence of Mera Peak and Island Peak will take you through the Amphulapsa Pass. It is a great trip at altitude that requires a good physical condition but also experience in the mountains.

Difficulties for Island Peak
Island Peak is a glacier high mountain route. Real mountaineering skills and a very good physical condition are essential. The summit ridge of Island Peak resembles the ridge of bumps at Mont Blanc.
It is necessary to be able to cling to the ascent and descent in slopes of about 45 Â° about 200 m high. The summit ridge is fine, although equipped with fixed ropes you have to be comfortable on this type of terrain.
You must be equipped with all your mountaineering equipment and know how to use it: metal cleats, ice axes, shoulder harness, 2 ice pins, 2 self-closing safety karabiners, jumar, descender, cord, mountain helmet necessary, frontal, a survival blanket, etc.

In all our circuits you have a free day the day after your arrival in Nepal or the return of your trek. We offer a day of discovery of Kathmandu. Browsing Bodhnath, Pashupatinath, Bhaktapur, Patan and the heart of the old city is always a change of scenery and a wonder.

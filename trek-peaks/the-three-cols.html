Crossing the 3 passes between Makalu and Khumbu



The three passes from Makalu to Khumbu and from Khumbu to Makalu

This crossing of the 3 passes connects the massive eastern Khumbu Hinku to the region of Makalu Baruntse Arun. It can be done in both directions Khumbu-Makalu or Makalu-Khumbu.
We crossed the 3 passes of Makalu Khumbu in trek peak because it is a difficult and committed route. This crossing is much more delicate than the classic peak treks. In addition to the altitude the engagement is important especially if the weather deteriorates.
A first route from Lukla Khumbu in which we will climb the Mera Peak with an altitude camp to 5800 m before crossing the 3 passes. At the top of the Méra we have one of the most beautiful views on the whole Himalayan range.
Then we will join the Baruntse base camp before climbing the West Pass. We will join the East Pass at the climb (40 ° slopes equipped with fixed ropes) then the descent of the East Pass will be equipped with fixed ropes by the Khumbu Shangrila team. All along we will have the Makalu in the background - grandiose.
 The end of the trek takes place in the valley of Makalu where we will descend the valleys of Barun and Arun to finish in the middle of rice paddies of Tashigaon and Num.
A second course offers you a departure from Tumlingtar Makalu to make this circuit in the other direction.
These treks are "engaged" there are glacier camps at high altitude, 5800 m, 5400 m and 6100 m as well as 40 ° slopes at the ascent and descent. More than a trek is a trip to the heart of the Himalayan massif and Nepal.

Difficulties crossing the 3 Cols
Crossing the 3 Cols is a glacier high mountain route. Real mountaineering skills and a very good physical condition are essential. It is necessary to be able to cling to the ascent and descent in slopes of about 45 ° about 200 m high. The climb to Col West and the descent of Col East although equipped with fixed ropes need to be comfortable on this type of slope.
You must be equipped with all your mountaineering equipment and know how to use it: metal cleats, ice axe, shoulder belt, 2 ice pins, 2 self closing snap hooks, jumar, descender, cord, necessary mountain helmet, frontal, a survival blanket, etc.

In all our circuits you have a free day the day after your arrival in Nepal or the return of your trek. We offer a day of discovery of Kathmandu. Browsing Bodhnath, Pashupatinath, Bhaktapur, Patan and the heart of the old city is always a change of scenery and a wonder.

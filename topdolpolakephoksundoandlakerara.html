Top Dolpo Lake Phoksundo and Lake Rara



Trekking sacred lakes Phoksumdo and Rara High Dolpo and Jomson

Between lakes and arid landscapes in this Tibetan enclave

Top Dolpo Lake Phoksundo and Lake Rara are 3 demanding treks because of their autonomy and their duration of more than 26 days of walking. They involve relatively long stages as well as 10 nights at more than 4,000 m and 8 passes over 5,000 m. Our walk is embellished with great panoramas and allows us meetings with the Dolpo-Pa inhabitants of Dolpo. We are in the heart of this region of Tibetan culture that still lives with the seasons and caravans. Life is organized around yaks, horses and trade with the nearby Tibet. The high hills, the harsh living conditions and the desert areas give this region of Nepal a fascinating beauty. Presence of many monasteries including that of Shey Gompa built in the 11th century and renovated a few years ago. Upper Dolpo is not a touristic region like the Annapurna Tower or the Khumbu. The lodges are non-existent, we sleep in tents, we wash in the rivers and our meals consist essentially of the traditional dish, the dalbhat. Bypassing Lake Rara by a wide lake-side trail in a very green meadow environment contrasts with the narrow corbelled path of Lake Phoksundo in a rather mineral and mountainous world built almost a thousand years ago. The geographical border between Dolpo and Haut Dolpo is the Dho Chharka line. North of this limit is Haut Dolpo. The moments of exchange and sharing remain magical moments . Drinking tea with the Yakmen we meet and driving caravans trading between Tibet and Nepal are fabulous moments. These treks fit perfectly well to the Tibetan maxim "the trip is a return to basics".


Highlights of Dolpo High treks

  • Rara Lake Shore Road
  • The path carved into the mountain above Lake Phoksundo
  • Departures of these treks in the greenery then the gradual climb to passes over 5000 m
  • Tibetan monasteries and culture from Saldang
  • The desert plateaus and the very mineral relief that contrast with the fields of wheat and millet
  • Low tourist area.

Kangchenjunga Trekking



Trek in the Kangchenjunga Valley

At the foot of a giant Himalayas

The Kangchenjunga trekking forms a loop. It is little frequented . Amazing variety of landscapes, moreover, in this region of Kangchenjunga you will not find cappuccinos like in the valley of Everest and probably you will not meet other trekkers or so very little. Altitude max 5100 m to Pangpema with a superb view of Kangchenjunga. The trek begins with the Cheram valley and then we pass a beautiful pass The Selele Pass to change the valley and meet in Ghunsa Kambachen.

Ghunsa in April is full of rhododendrons. From Kambachen the landscapes are desert and exceptional view on the Jannu. These are the great wild areas close to Tibet and Sikkim . Kambachen is a huge amphitheater with views of Jannu and Kangchenjunga. Red Panda says there are about 134 pandas in the Suketar area. The Kangchenjunga is the 3rd highest mountain in the world at 8585 m.

Located on the border in Nepal and Sikkim it is also the highest peak of India.The return is by Pathibhara a place of Hindu pilgrimage with the night passed 3800 m next to the temple of Pathibhara to attend the sunset and the sunrise on the Himalayan chain from the Sikkim to Makalu and Everest through Kangchenjunga.


Highlights of the treks on the Tour du Manaslu

  • Very different landscapes throughout the trek
  • Exceptional views of Jannu and Kangchenjunga
  • Contacts with the inhabitants
  • The real impression of being far from everything
  • The passage of the Col de Mirgin La
  • The villages of peasants and shepherds of Ghunsa, Kambachen and Lhonak
  • The return by the temple of Pathibhara for the sight and the serenity of the place
